Resources for Suicide

You matter. You are loved. Suicide is one of the leading causes of death in the United States. It takes a lot of courage to reach out and talk about these kinds of emotions. If you or a loved one is in crisis, please click the button below to receive help from the National Suicide Prevention Lifeline.

DISCLAIMER: We can not give medical or therapuetic advice. As such, we strongly encourage you to utilize the resources at the bottom of the page.

Signs The Someone May be Thinking About Suicide

If you notice someone doing the following, they might be considering suicide. This is not an extensive list. Someone who shows these signs is not always suicidal and someone who may be considering suicide does not always show these signs. Be aware if someone:

  • Talks more about:
    • death
    • being a burden
    • feeling trapped
    • different ways of dying
    • not seeing a reason to live
  • Gives up on things they normally enjoy
  • Struggles to focus on tasks at hand
  • Sleeps too much or too little
  • Isolates themselves

Professional Resources

  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline is a confidential, free, 24/7/365 lifeline for people in crisis. It's very brave to step up and ask for help.
